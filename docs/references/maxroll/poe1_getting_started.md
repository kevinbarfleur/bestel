# Maxroll — Getting Started Catalog (Path of Exile 1)

Source: <https://maxroll.gg/poe/category/getting-started>

This catalog covers the 21 Maxroll articles aimed at new and returning players. The page is organized in two toggles on Maxroll: **Beginner Guides** (13 articles covering core mechanics and campaign play) and **Atlas Guide** (8 articles covering the endgame Atlas system).

For each article, the agent gets a description, the topics covered, and a routing hint to know when to consult it.

---

## Toggle 1 — Beginner Guides

### 1. First Steps for Beginners

**URL:** <https://maxroll.gg/poe/getting-started/first-steps>

**What it covers:** A complete onboarding article for someone opening PoE for the first time. It walks through league selection (temporary softcore vs. hardcore vs. standard), the seven base classes and their ascendancies, the philosophy of "any class can do anything," the importance of following a build guide as a first character, and a bird's-eye view of currency, skill gems, support gems, sockets, links, and the passive tree. It also covers monetization (free-to-play with cosmetic and stash-tab microtransactions) and explains why specialised stash tabs become valuable in endgame.

**Topics covered:**
- Choice of league (temporary, standard, hardcore, ruthless)
- The 7 classes and the Scion unlock
- Ascendancy concept introduced
- Skills as gems, supports, sockets and links
- First overview of currency and trade
- Why following a build guide matters early

**When to consult:** Player is brand-new to PoE, asks "where do I start," confuses class power with class restriction, or doesn't understand why people pick specific leagues.

### 2. Options & Key Binds Guide

Walks through the skill bar (8 main slots + 5 CTRL slots), key binds for skills, the critical "Always Attack Without Moving" toggle (per-skill), life-bar visibility settings, map tuning, cursor options, and which audio settings to enable for boss voice cues.

**Topics covered:**
- Skill bar setup and CTRL bar
- Always Attack Without Moving (must-enable for most skills)
- Per-skill rebinding when swapping gems
- Health-bar visibility for self, allies, monsters
- Map zoom, cursor highlighting, tuning sound

**When to consult:** Player complains about character moving when they cast/attack, asks how to bind skills, can't see enemy health bars, or asks generally how to set up the UI for the first time.

### 3. Campaign Guide (All 10 Acts Walkthrough)

**URL:** <https://maxroll.gg/poe/getting-started/campaign-guide>

**What it covers:** A full act-by-act walkthrough of PoE's 10-act campaign. Each act is broken down zone by zone with optimal pathing, mandatory quests, optional quests that grant passive points (de-facto required), boss strategies (Hillock, Brutus, Merveil, Vaal Oversoul, Piety, Dominus, the bandit choice, Kaom, Daresso, Kitava, etc.), Recipe Altar locations for the Crafting Bench, Trial of Ascendancy locations for unlocking the Labyrinth, and resistance-capping milestones tied to act progression.

**Topics covered:**
- Detailed zone-by-zone pathing for Acts 1–10
- Mandatory vs. optional quests and which give passive skill points
- Boss strategies for every act boss
- Bandit quest (Alira / Oak / Kraityn) decision
- Trial of Ascendancy locations
- Pantheon power unlocks per act
- Resistance breakpoints (75% cap by Act 5 / Act 10 Kitava)
- Recipe Altar locations to extend the Crafting Bench

**When to consult:** Player is leveling and asks where something is, which quest matters, how to beat a campaign boss, where to find a trial, when to cap resistances. Also useful when a player says "I'm stuck in Act X."

### 4. Power Up Guide for Beginners

**URL:** <https://maxroll.gg/poe/getting-started/powering-up>

**What it covers:** What to do when a leveling character feels weak. Covers vendor recipes (item combinations sold to the vendor for specific outcomes, available from level 8+), the Crafting Bench (unlocked in Act 2 after the *Safe and Sound* quest, used for capping attribute requirements and resistances), Essences (tier-based crafting materials that turn normal items into rares with a guaranteed mod), and leveling uniques that provide power spikes. Also discusses the importance of weapon upgrades for attack-based builds (vs. gem-level scaling for spell builds).

**Topics covered:**
- Vendor recipes for specific item outcomes
- Crafting Bench mechanics and modifier costs
- Essences as a power tool during the campaign
- Key leveling uniques and how to acquire them
- Why weapon base damage matters for attack builds
- When to overcommit vs. not overcommit currency

**When to consult:** Player says they feel weak in the campaign, asks how to get more damage during leveling, wants to know what to do with their currency, or doesn't understand why their attack build "feels bad" without a good weapon.

### 6. Defense Guide for Beginners

**URL:** <https://maxroll.gg/poe/getting-started/defenses-for-beginners>

**What it covers:** A comprehensive breakdown of all defensive layers. Covers life pool scaling (Strength giving +5 life per 10, %increased Maximum Life, +to Maximum Life), the 75% elemental resistance cap (and how to push to 90% via max-res mods), chaos resistance, Armor (effective against many small hits, weak vs. big hits), Evasion (chance-based avoidance for attacks only), Energy Shield (recharge after no-damage period, vulnerable to chaos), Ward, and how flasks integrate. It also covers behavioral defense — never stand still, don't backtrack, beware on-death effects, and which map modifiers to avoid.

**Topics covered:**
- Life pool sources and scaling
- Elemental resistance cap and overcapping
- Chaos resistance specifics
- Armor vs. Evasion vs. ES vs. Ward — when each works
- Spell suppression as a Dexterity-side defense
- Stun avoidance via ES
- Behavioral defense (positioning, on-death effects)
- Map modifiers to avoid

**When to consult:** Player keeps dying, asks why they can't survive one-shots, doesn't understand why their armor isn't helping, or asks about resistance capping. Also good for any "what defense should I use" question.

### 8. Attribute & Socket Colour Guide for Beginners

**URL:** <https://maxroll.gg/poe/getting-started/attributes-for-beginners>

**What it covers:** How attributes (Strength, Dexterity, Intelligence) drive socket colour probabilities and item-base requirements. Each attribute is tied to a defense type (STR=Armor, DEX=Evasion, INT=Energy Shield) and a socket colour (red/green/blue). High-attribute-requirement bases bias toward matching socket colours when using Chromatic Orbs. Hybrid bases split requirements and roll mixed colours more easily. Also covers minor offensive/defensive bonuses each attribute provides directly.

**Topics covered:**
- STR/DEX/INT and their defense pairings
- Socket colour probability based on item attribute requirements
- Hybrid bases (e.g., Sadist Garb) for mixed colours
- Chromatic Orbs and Vorici colour-tactics
- Direct attribute bonuses (life from STR, evasion from DEX, mana from INT)

**When to consult:** Player asks why they can't get certain socket colours, doesn't understand why a "mostly INT" body armor refuses to roll red sockets, or asks what each attribute actually does.

**Topics covered:**

- Premium stash tabs (cost ~$5 minimum to unlock listing)
- Whisper-based trade workflow
- Chaos Orb and Divine Orb as core trade units
- Trade etiquette and party invite flow
- Buy orders vs. sell listings

**When to consult:** Player asks how to sell items, how to buy something on trade, why they can't list items, what a Chaos / Divine ratio is, or how the in-game currency exchange works.

### 16. Atlas Passive Tree Explained

**URL:** <https://maxroll.gg/poe/getting-started/atlas-passive-tree>

**What it covers:** The structure and history of the Atlas Passive Tree. Originally introduced in 3.13 (Echoes of the Atlas) as multiple per-region trees, reworked in 3.17 (Siege of the Atlas) into a single unified tree. Up to 132 points available: 115 from Map Completion Bonuses + 17 from Atlas Books of Skill (rewarded by killing Pinnacle Bosses and their sub-bosses for the first time, and from Maven's Invitation: The Atlas). Refund via Orb of Unmaking. The tree contains Notables (cluster ends, often mechanic-themed), Keystones (transformative), and small travel nodes. Also covers strategic priority — Kirac, easy mechanics first (Bestiary, Ambush, Harbinger, Heist, Essence), then Eater/Exarch wheels for influenced monster density.

**Topics covered:**
- History of the Atlas Tree (3.13, 3.17, current)
- Total point count and sources
- Notables vs. Keystones vs. travel nodes
- Refund via Orb of Unmaking (vendor recipe with Orb of Regret)
- The `/atlaspassives` command to check missing points
- Strategic node-priority order

**When to consult:** Player asks how the Atlas Tree works, what Voidstones unlock, where to spend points first, or how many points exist total. Also useful for returning players who missed reworks.

### 17. Atlas Questlines Explained

**URL:** <https://maxroll.gg/poe/getting-started/questlines>

**What it covers:** The three overlapping questlines triggered at yellow maps. **Maven's questline** — the Maven witnesses your boss kills, fills the Maven Progression Bar, eventually leads to Maven's Invitations and the Maven boss fight (rewards Ceremonial Voidstone). **Searing Exarch questline** — kill the Black Star (Polaric Invitation) then the Searing Exarch (Incandescent Invitation, rewards Omniscient Voidstone). **Eater of Worlds questline** — kill the Infinite Hunger (Writhing Invitation) then the Eater of Worlds (Screaming Invitation, rewards Grasping Voidstone). Each questline is bound to its Beacon being selected on the Map Device. Quest invitations become tradeable items after the first kill.

**Topics covered:**
- Maven's questline (boss progression, Crucible, Writ, Maven fight)
- Searing Exarch questline (Black Star → Exarch)
- Eater of Worlds questline (Infinite Hunger → Eater)
- Beacon selection mechanic on Map Device
- How influenced maps progress the count (14th and 28th boss drops)
- Atlas passives that double progression chance (Baptised by Fire, Etched by Acid)

**When to consult:** Player asks how to fight a Pinnacle Boss, how to drop an Invitation, what a Beacon does, or what the Maven's bar is. Also good for "how do I unlock Voidstones."

### 21. Endgame Activities

**URL:** <https://maxroll.gg/poe/getting-started/endgame-activities>

**What it covers:** A taxonomy of endgame play styles. Covers Mapping (default, with sub-styles like Alch-and-Go, Juicing, Loot Tile farming), Bossing (farming Pinnacle and sub-Pinnacle bosses for chase uniques, fragment requirements), Heist (Grand Heist contracts and blueprints for unique markers), Labyrinth (gem mods and enchants), Delve (infinitely deep Azurite Mine via Sulphite, requires specialised builds), Simulacrum (30-wave endgame encounter), Uber-Blight (tower defense), Crafting (selling crafted items for profit), and Market Play (currency flipping, bulk selling). Notes that profitability fluctuates heavily between leagues.

**Topics covered:**
- Mapping styles (alch-and-go, juicing, loot tiles, MF)
- Bossing for unique drops
- Heist (Grand Heist focus)
- Labyrinth as endgame (gem rewards, enchants)
- Delve mechanics overview
- Simulacrum 30-wave structure
- Uber-Blight tower defense
- Crafting and market activities
- Valdo's Maps as challenge content

**When to consult:** Player has finished mapping for the first time and asks "what else can I do," wants to specialise in a non-mapping playstyle, or asks about a specific endgame mechanic at a high level.

**Recency notes:** Profitability of each activity is highly meta-dependent and shifts each league. Use this article for the structural overview, not for which activity is "best right now."
