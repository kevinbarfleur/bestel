# Maxroll — Bosses Catalog (Path of Exile 1)

Source: <https://maxroll.gg/poe/category/bosses>

This catalog lists Maxroll's individual boss guides. The agent should use these to answer questions about a specific boss fight (mechanics, abilities, phases, loot table, how to access the encounter, Uber variant differences). For broader Atlas/Pinnacle progression questions, use `maxroll_getting_started.md` (Atlas Questlines).

The bosses are grouped by **boss family** (Pinnacle, Conquerors, Shaper Guardians, Elder Guardians, Breachlords, Synthesis, Originator, Mastermind). Each entry covers what the article contains and when to consult it.

## Pinnacle Bosses (Voidstone-granting)

These four are the most important endgame bosses — defeating them grants Voidstones that raise all map tiers up to T16. They sit at the heart of Atlas progression.

### 1. The Maven

**URL:** <https://maxroll.gg/poe/bosses/the-maven-boss-guide>

**What it covers:** The Maven encounter — main antagonist of Echoes of the Atlas (3.13). Accessed via the Maven's Writ (10 Crescent Splinters from completed Maven's Crucible witnesses). Defeating her grants the Ceremonial Voidstone. Article documents her abilities (Volatile Orbs, Tri-Beam Claws on "STAND STILL!", Cascade of Pain, Sun Projectile on "TAKE THIS!", Brain Beams from the Nucleus phase, Memory Game phase, Circle Slam on "DO WHAT I SAY!"), strategy tips (stack Gravity Wells in arena center, never touch Brain Beams, focus on Memory Game), and Uber Maven changes (5 Reality Fragments, persistent void zones, +4 projectiles, ramped damage). Includes loot table (Legacy of Fury, Graven's Secret, Arn's Anguish, Olesya's Delight, Doppelgänger Guise, Echoforge, Awakened Support Gems, Orb of Conflict).

**When to consult:** Player asks "how do I beat the Maven," wants Maven mechanics broken down, asks how to access the fight, or asks about Memory Game phase. Also for Uber Maven prep.

---

### 2. Sirus, Awakener of Worlds

**URL:** <https://maxroll.gg/poe/bosses/sirus-awakener-of-worlds-boss-guide>

**What it covers:** The leader of the Elderslayers (Conqueror's of the Atlas, 3.9). Accessed via Crest of the Elderslayers (4 Conqueror crests). Defeating him grants a Favoured Map Slot. Article details his Die Beam ("DIE!" voice line, 3 beams in early phases, 4 tracking beams in Phase 4), Corridor Blast (chaos walls), Storm of Corruption (apocalypse phase with rotating beams), Meteor Maze (1/2/3 rings depending on phase, find exit before meteor lands), and Multi-Clone Beam in Phase 4 (12 clones, only Sirus has a health bar). Strategy emphasis on Corrupted Blood immunity (flask, mastery, or jewel implicit) and listening to voice lines. Uber Sirus uses 5 Awakening Fragments and adds tracking storms, faster Meteor Maze, persistent Desolation, 6-beam centred ability, and tracking Multi-Clone Beam.

**When to consult:** Player asks how to beat Sirus, what the Die Beam is, how to dodge the maze, why they're dying to Corrupted Blood, or asks about Uber Sirus drop differences.

---

### 3. The Searing Exarch

**URL:** <https://maxroll.gg/poe/bosses/the-searing-exarch-boss-guide>

**What it covers:** One of the four Eldritch Horrors (Siege of the Atlas, 3.17). Drops the Omniscient Voidstone. Accessed via the Incandescent Invitation (drop after Black Star kill in Exarch-influenced T14+ maps, 28th map drops it; Atlas passive *Baptised by Fire* doubles progression chance). Article covers his abilities — Flame Wall on "Disintegration!", Blazing Salvo on "Annihilation!" (channeled, follows player), Lantern Explosion on "Incineration!" (charged AoE bypassing dodge/block), Rolling Meteor Wall on "The fires of a thousand suns!" (the hardest phase, Maven assists by punching gaps), Searing Rune Patterns (debuff on contact). Tips include Quicksilver Flask, Ruby Flask for Meteor phase, blocking meteors with minions/totems/Frost Wall. Uber adds Molten Minotaur per meteor wave, 16 lines of meteors per phase.

**When to consult:** Player asks how to beat Searing Exarch, asks what the meteor walls are, why they're getting one-shot by Lantern Explosion, or wants to know how to block meteors.

---

### 4. The Eater of Worlds

**URL:** <https://maxroll.gg/poe/bosses/the-eater-of-worlds-boss-guide>

**What it covers:** Counterpart to Searing Exarch — drops the Grasping Voidstone. Accessed via Screaming Invitation (drop after Infinite Hunger kill in Eater-influenced T14+ maps, 28th map; Atlas passive *Etched by Acid* doubles progression). Article covers his abilities — Tentacle Slams (default attack), Lightning Beam (stacking debuff, "Inescapable Doom"), and the signature Doom Phase ("Inescapable doom!" → "All will be devoured!"): Maven spawns Energy Spheres, player has ~20 seconds to step on all of them or take a lethal explosion. Tips include Dash/Flame Dash to teleport between spheres, Quicksilver Flask, sound-on for voice cues. Uber Eater adds wider Tentacle Rings, Doom Phase max spheres, faster horror orbs, 70% less damage on monsters during fight.

**When to consult:** Player asks how to beat Eater of Worlds, how to handle the Doom Phase, what activates the spheres, or asks for Uber Eater differences.

## Atlas Legacy Bosses (Shaper / Elder line)

These bosses still exist as challenge content even after their lore was overwritten by Sirus. They're farmed for boss-specific loot and Watcher's Eye drops.

### 7. The Shaper

**URL:** <https://maxroll.gg/poe/bosses/the-shaper-boss-guide>

**What it covers:** Creator of the Atlas, introduced in 2.4 (Essence League). Accessed via Key to the Crucible (4 fragments from Shaper Guardians: Phoenix, Chimera, Hydra, Minotaur). Article covers his iconic abilities — the Shaper Beam ("Gaze into the Abyss"), Slam combo (Teleport Slam, lethal to most builds), Volatile Orbs, Bullet Hell phase (Zana protects with shield), Vortex Zones (cold DoT on contact). Notes that he doesn't deal Chaos or Bleed. Includes Uber Shaper differences (5 Cosmic Fragments, two clones at start, no Zana protection in Bullet Hell, persistent vortex zones). Lore tie-in: Shaper's real name is Valdo Caeserius, father of Zana.

**When to consult:** Player asks how to beat Shaper, asks what the Shaper Beam does, or asks about the Watcher's Eye drop.

---

### 8. The Elder

**URL:** <https://maxroll.gg/poe/bosses/the-elder-boss-guide>

**What it covers:** Antagonist of War for the Atlas (3.1, Abyss League). Accessed via Key to Decay (4 fragments from Elder Guardians: Purifier, Constrictor, Enslaver, Eradicator). Considered the easiest of the major Atlas bosses but still dangerous. Article covers his Ice Spear variants (3 different patterns), Siphon attacks (regular and ground-degen), Ring of Death (expanding ring, must stay inside center for final explosion), Tentacle Slams. The encounter has a dramatic Phase 2 where Shaper binds the Elder while you protect Shaper from Null Portal monsters. Drops the 2-mod Watcher's Eye.

**When to consult:** Player asks how to beat Elder, wants to farm Watcher's Eyes, or asks how to access Uber Elder.

---

### 9. The Uber Elder

**URL:** <https://maxroll.gg/poe/bosses/the-uber-elder-boss-guide>

**What it covers:** Dual encounter — Shaper + Elder fought simultaneously. Final boss of War for the Atlas (3.2 Bestiary League). Accessed via the Maddening Object (4 fragments: 2 from Elder, 2 from Shaper). Drops the Decayed Voidstone (rewarded first-time only). Article describes the 7-phase structure (4 boss phases, 3 intermissions): you can only attack Shaper while he's above 75%, then Elder becomes vulnerable while Shaper shields. The fight is considered one of the hardest in PoE — boss abilities overlap. The 3-mod Watcher's Eye drops here. Uber Uber Elder uses 5 Decaying Fragments, adds vastly higher life/damage, faster slams, additional yellow ball projectiles, and a Bullet Hell variant without Zana's protective shield.

**When to consult:** Player asks how to beat Uber Elder, wants 3-mod Watcher's Eye, or doesn't understand the dual-boss invulnerability mechanic.

## Shaper Guardians (Guardians of the Void)

Each drops one of the four fragments needed to access Shaper. Always T16, fought as the boss of their dedicated maps.

### 10. Guardian of the Phoenix

**URL:** <https://maxroll.gg/poe/bosses/guardian-of-the-phoenix-boss-guide>

**What it covers:** Forge of the Phoenix map boss. Drops Fragment of the Phoenix. Article covers his Firebomb (lethal AoE, must dodge), the stacking debuff that reduces max Fire Resistance by 1% per 10% boss HP lost (encounter gets progressively harder), the importance of Fire Resistance overcap, and Atlas Passive bonuses for Shaper Guardian maps.

**When to consult:** Player asks how to beat Phoenix, why their fire resistance keeps dropping, or how to farm Phoenix.

---

### 11. Guardian of the Chimera

**URL:** <https://maxroll.gg/poe/bosses/guardian-of-the-chimera-boss-guide>

**What it covers:** Pit of the Chimera map boss. Drops Fragment of the Chimera. Article details the two-phase loop (Boss Phase + Minion Phase): Chimera uses Flicker Strike, Combo Attack, Charge Attack, Charged Slash (with bleed), then teleports out and spawns waves of minions. Each minion wave must be fully defeated to progress. The fourth wave includes a unique mini-boss. Three minion sets spawn in random order.

**When to consult:** Player asks how to beat Chimera, why she keeps disappearing, or how to handle the minion phase.

### 12. Guardian of the Hydra

**URL:** <https://maxroll.gg/poe/bosses/guardian-of-the-hydra-boss-guide>

**What it covers:** Lair of the Hydra map boss. Drops Fragment of the Hydra. Article covers her cold projectile barrages (Barrage, Fork Arrow, Doom Arrow), Frostbolts fired from arena cannons, mini-boss minion spawns during teleport phases, and the importance of side-stepping projectile lines.

**When to consult:** Player asks how to beat Hydra or asks about projectile dodging.

### 13. Guardian of the Minotaur

**URL:** <https://maxroll.gg/poe/bosses/guardian-of-the-minotaur-boss-guide>

**What it covers:** Maze of the Minotaur map boss. Drops Fragment of the Minotaur. Considered one of the hardest Guardian fights without high DPS. Article covers his Sweep ability (Lightning AoE), Burrow ability (one-shots, must run perpendicular to current position), Slam attack with falling rocks and Stone Golem spawns, and Lightning Walls that block paths and apply shock + slow + DoT on contact (use Flame Dash to bypass).

**When to consult:** Player asks how to beat Minotaur, how to dodge the Burrow, or how to handle Lightning Walls.

## Cross-reference notes

- Pinnacle bosses unlock Voidstones — the four required are Maven (Ceremonial), Searing Exarch (Omniscient), Eater of Worlds (Grasping), and Sirus (granted on Conqueror progression, but the Decayed Voidstone comes from Uber Elder). For details, see `maxroll_getting_started.md` → Atlas Questlines.
- Each boss family is gated behind specific currency/fragment sets — Pinnacle Bosses use Invitations (Maven's Writ, Polaric/Incandescent, Writhing/Screaming, Crest of the Elderslayers), Atlas Legacy uses fragment sets (Key to Crucible, Key to Decay, Maddening Object), Breachlords use Breachstones, Cortex uses Synthesised Maps.
- Maven's Invitations regroup multiple bosses into a single fight — the Hidden (Breachlords + Catarina), the Feared (multi-boss including Originator Incarnations and Cortex post-3.28), the Forgotten, the Atlas, the Twisted/Atlas. Cross-check the latest patch for current invitation rosters.
